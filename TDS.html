Key Features of TDS

Understand the essential features of Tax Deducted at Source (TDS)

💰
TDS Deduction

TDS is deducted at the time of making specified payments such as salary, interest, rent, commission, etc.

📑
TDS Filing

Every deductor is required to file quarterly TDS returns to the government.

📋
TAN Requirement

A Tax Deduction and Collection Account Number (TAN) is mandatory to deduct TDS.

⚖️
TDS on Salary

Employers deduct TDS from salaries based on the income tax slab applicable to employees.

📝
Form 16 & 26AS

Form 16 is issued to employees, and Form 26AS provides a consolidated view of TDS deductions.

🔎
TDS Exemptions

Exemptions are available for specific transactions if a valid declaration is submitted.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about TDS Registration.

TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) registration is required for entities and individuals responsible for deducting tax at source when making specified payments such as salaries, rent, or professional fees.

The required documents include:
  • ✔ PAN Card of the deductor
  • ✔ Aadhaar Card (for individuals)
  • ✔ Business registration proof (for companies & firms)
  • ✔ Bank account details
  • ✔ Address proof (utility bill, lease agreement, etc.)

TDS registration is typically processed within 7-10 working days upon submission of all required documents.

TDS registration provides multiple benefits, including:
  • ✔ Compliance with tax regulations
  • ✔ Avoidance of penalties for non-deduction
  • ✔ Timely tax payments reduce financial risks
  • ✔ Helps in accurate tax reporting

TDS registration is required for:
  • ✔ Employers deducting TDS from employee salaries
  • ✔ Businesses making payments subject to TDS
  • ✔ Individuals & firms (if turnover exceeds the prescribed limit)
  • ✔ Government offices responsible for tax deductions
