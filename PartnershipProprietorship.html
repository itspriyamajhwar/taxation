Proprietor to Private Company Conversion Procedure

Obtain DSC & DIN

Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) and Director Identification Number (DIN) are mandatory for directors.

Name Approval

Apply for a unique company name through the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA).

Drafting MOA & AOA

Memorandum of Association (MOA) and Articles of Association (AOA) must be prepared and filed.

File Registration Forms

Submit incorporation documents to the MCA, including SPICe+ form.

Obtain PAN & TAN

Apply for the company’s Permanent Account Number (PAN) and Tax Deduction Account Number (TAN).

Company Registration

Once verified, MCA issues a Certificate of Incorporation, and the conversion is complete.

Partnership & Proprietorship Registration

Complete guide to registering your business as a Partnership or Sole Proprietorship.

Partnership Registration

A partnership firm is a business entity where two or more individuals agree to share profits and responsibilities. It requires a **Partnership Deed** and registration under the **Partnership Act, 1932**.

  • Minimum 2 partners
  • No minimum capital required
  • PAN and GST registration needed

Proprietorship Registration

A Sole Proprietorship is a simple business structure owned by one person. It requires fewer compliances and is ideal for small businesses.

  • Single Owner
  • Easy to start & operate
  • Requires GST, MSME, and PAN registration

Registration Process

The registration process includes:

  1. Choose Business Structure (Partnership or Proprietorship)
  2. Apply for GST & PAN
  3. Prepare Partnership Deed (for Partnership Firms)
  4. Register under **MSME Act** for benefits
  5. Open a Business Bank Account
