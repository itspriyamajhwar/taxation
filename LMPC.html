What is LMPC Certificate?

The Legal Metrology Packaged Commodities (LMPC) Certificate is a registration required under the Legal Metrology Act, 2009 for businesses that import or manufacture packaged goods. It ensures proper declaration of weight, measure, packaging, labeling, and pricing on consumer products. LMPC registration is mandatory for all importers and domestic manufacturers of pre-packaged commodities.

Benefits of LMPC Registration

  • Ensures compliance with Legal Metrology Rules
  • Avoids penalties or confiscation by customs or authorities
  • Improves credibility and consumer trust
  • Facilitates smooth clearance at ports for importers
  • Enables nationwide distribution of packaged products

Documents Required for LMPC Certificate

  • Company PAN & GST Certificate
  • Importer Exporter Code (IEC)
  • List of products to be imported or manufactured
  • Product images and packaging details
  • Address proof of business premises
  • Declaration letter & authorization from the company
  • Copy of the Import/Trade license (if applicable)

Procedure for LMPC Registration

Step 1: Identify applicability of LMPC based on your products
Step 2: Collect and verify required documents
Step 3: File application with the Legal Metrology Department
Step 4: Submit hard copy and fees (if required)
Step 5: Follow-up for verification and inspection (if any)
Step 6: Receive LMPC Certificate

Who Needs an LMPC Certificate?

  • Importers of packaged goods
  • Manufacturers and packers of consumer goods
  • Retailers dealing in pre-packaged commodities
  • Exporters (if product re-enters Indian market)

Validity and Renewal

The LMPC Certificate is generally valid for a period of one to five years, depending on the department norms. It must be renewed before the expiry to avoid penalties. Renewal requires updated documents and continued compliance with labeling standards.
