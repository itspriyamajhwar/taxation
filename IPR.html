What is IPR (Intellectual Property Rights)?

Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) protect the creations of the human mind including inventions, designs, literary and artistic works, and brand names. Registration provides exclusive rights to the creator and legal protection from unauthorized use or duplication.

Types of IPR We Help With

Trademark

Protect logos, brand names, slogans, and other business identifiers to ensure exclusivity and avoid misuse.

Copyright

Secure your artistic and literary works such as music, books, videos, and software from unauthorized use.

Patent

Register your novel inventions and processes for exclusive rights to use, sell, or license them legally.

Why IPR Registration is Important

  • Protects intellectual creations and prevents copying or misuse
  • Builds brand value and legal ownership
  • Enables monetization through licensing and royalties
  • Enhances competitiveness and credibility in the market

Procedure for IPR Registration

  1. Consultation and IPR type selection
  2. Document preparation and application drafting
  3. Filing with appropriate government authority
  4. Examination and response to objections (if any)
  5. Final approval and certificate issuance

Documents Required

  • Identity & address proof of applicant
  • Proof of ownership or originality
  • Logo/image (for trademark)
  • Detailed description of invention (for patent)
  • Soft copy of creative work (for copyright)
