What is RERA?

RERA (Real Estate Regulatory Authority) is a regulatory body established under the Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016. It aims to regulate and promote the real estate sector, ensuring transparency, accountability, and timely completion of projects.

Why RERA Registration is Important

Legal Compliance

RERA registration is mandatory for builders and developers to operate legally and gain buyer trust.

Transparency & Accountability

RERA ensures buyers are protected from project delays and misleading advertisements.

Procedure for RERA Registration

  1. Fill and submit the RERA application form online.
  2. Upload the necessary documents and declarations.
  3. Pay the government fees as applicable.
  4. Verification and approval by the authority.
  5. Receive your RERA registration number and certificate.

Documents Required for RERA Registration

  • PAN Card of the Promoter
  • Project details including layout and plan approvals
  • Legal title deed of the land
  • Financial documents of the promoter
  • Encumbrance Certificate (if applicable)

Frequently Asked Questions

Any builder, promoter, or developer of a residential/commercial project exceeding 500 sq. meters or 8 units must register under RERA.

Non-registration attracts heavy penalties and may lead to legal consequences or cancellation of the project.
