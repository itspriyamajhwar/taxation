What is Digital Signature (DSC)?

A Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) is an electronic form of a signature that allows users to sign documents digitally. It is legally valid and ensures data integrity and authenticity in online transactions or document submissions, especially for income tax, MCA filings, e-tenders, and more.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

A Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) is an electronic signature that validates the identity of the signer and ensures the integrity of digital documents.

Individuals or businesses that need to file returns with MCA, Income Tax, GST, or participate in e-tendering processes are required to obtain a DSC.

A Digital Signature Certificate is usually issued with a validity of 1 or 2 years and must be renewed before expiry.

The DSC is usually issued within 1 to 2 working days after completing the application and KYC verification process.

PAN card, Aadhaar card, passport-size photo, and email/phone verification are usually required for individuals. Organizations may need authorization letters and proof of company registration.
