What is an EPR Certificate?

Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) is an environmental policy approach in which producers are responsible for the entire lifecycle of the products they introduce in the market, especially for their take-back, recycling, and final disposal. The EPR Certificate is mandatory for businesses involved in electronic waste (e-waste), plastic waste, batteries, and other polluting materials.

Benefits of EPR Registration

  • Compliance with environmental laws and regulations
  • Improved brand credibility
  • Access to environmentally conscious markets
  • Better waste management and recycling practices
  • Prevention of penalties for non-compliance

Documents Required for EPR Registration

  • Company PAN and GST Certificate
  • Certificate of Incorporation
  • Authorized Signatory Aadhaar & PAN
  • Details of Products & Categories
  • Waste Management Plan
  • Agreement with Recycler (if applicable)
  • Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)

Procedure for Getting EPR Certificate

Step 1: Assess applicability based on your product line
Step 2: Collect and prepare required documents
Step 3: Register on CPCB portal (Central Pollution Control Board)
Step 4: Submit application and documents
Step 5: Follow up for approval/clarifications
Step 6: Receive EPR Authorization Certificate

Who Needs EPR Authorization?

  • Electronics & Electrical Equipment Manufacturers
  • Plastic Packaging Producers
  • Battery Manufacturers
  • Importers of packaged goods or e-waste
  • Brand owners and e-commerce businesses

Validity & Compliance

The EPR Certificate is valid for a specified period as defined by the CPCB or State PCB. Renewal must be done before the expiry. Regular compliance reports, recycling proof, and audit records must be submitted annually or as directed by the authority.
